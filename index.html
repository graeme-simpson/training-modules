Conflicts of Interest:
What Every Trustee Must Know

This module forms part of your formal trustee knowledge and understanding (TKU) programme, as required by The Pensions Act 2004 and the Pensions Regulator's General Code of Practice. Completing it will help you identify, disclose, and manage conflicts of interest in your role as a defined benefit pension scheme trustee.

Regulatory basis: Section 247 of the Pensions Act 2004 requires trustees to be conversant with the scheme's trust deed and rules, Statement of Investment Principles, and other key documents. The Pensions Regulator's General Code (in force February 2024) sets out expectations for an Effective System of Governance (ESOG) that includes documented procedures for managing conflicts of interest. Trustees have a general law fiduciary duty to act in the best interests of beneficiaries, free from the influence of personal or third-party interests.
